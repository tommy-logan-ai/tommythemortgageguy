The weighted reconciliation — giving the most credit to Parkland and Bellshire, the two strongest comps — lands at $570,000 as your most probable sale price. The Windsor and Arlington sales adjusted out considerably below the cluster and were weighted lightly; their price-per-square-foot suggests either condition issues or a different micro-location dynamic that doesn't translate directly to Essex Drive.

If you ever do decide to list: pricing at $569,900–$579,900 puts you in a competitive window that attracts serious buyers without leaving money on the table. Rochester Hills is moving fast right now — the five comps averaged just 14 days on market — so an aggressive list price is defensible if the home shows well.

Your biggest unrealized lever is your bath count. At this price tier, buyers expect 3 full baths. If a master bath addition or a basement full bath is on the table before listing, that single upgrade could close the gap to the $600K ceiling — and then some.
