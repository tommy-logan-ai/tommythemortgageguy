The data lands between $399K and $427K on a straight adjusted-value basis. But this comp set is thin, and it doesn't fully capture what you've done to this property — a full renovation in a rural market where most buyers are acquiring unrenovated product. A list price of $439,900 is aggressive but defensible: it's priced above the comp-adjusted range as a reflection of genuine condition superiority, while staying under the psychological $440K ceiling that keeps you competitive with buyers using conventional financing.

If you list at $439,900, you should be mentally prepared for offers in the $420K–$435K range, which still represents a strong return on your renovation investment. The bigger risk in this market isn't overpricing by $10K — it's sitting. Rural St. Clair County buyer pools are thin, and every week of DOM chips away at leverage. Price to sell in 30–45 days, not to negotiate forever.

The single biggest lever you have right now is presentation. Your improvements are real, but rural buyers and rural appraisers need to see them clearly. Before you go live: professional photography, a clean shot of every outbuilding, and a one-page renovation summary to hand to any appraiser who walks through. That document could be the difference between an appraisal that hits and one that kills a deal at the finish line.

Greenwood Township is a thin, low-inventory rural market. We're not talking about a subdivision with 40 sales a year — we're talking about 2–3 comparable ranch sales in a 12-month window. That's actually good news for a well-prepared seller: there's almost nothing else to buy. When a qualified buyer wants a renovated ranch on acreage in this area, yours may be the only option.

The price trend in this tier is flat to modestly positive. The Yale Rd comps both sold in the $352K–$402K range, which is where the market has been. The upward movement in this market isn't driven by speculative demand — it's driven by scarcity. As long as interest rates stay elevated and new construction in rural St. Clair County remains cost-prohibitive, supply will stay tight and serious buyers will pay for move-in-ready condition.

Sebastian, here's the honest takeaway: your home is worth significantly more than it was when you bought it, and the data backs that up. The market is thin, which creates both an opportunity and a risk — price it right, present it well, and you'll attract the buyer who's been waiting for exactly this. Price it too high without the presentation to match, and you'll sit. The plan here is to do both right.
