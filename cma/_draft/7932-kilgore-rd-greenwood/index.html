The weighted reconciliation lands at $440,000 — and it gets there honestly. The prior sale of your own home at $370K is the cleanest possible baseline. When you add the full-bath addition ($12K), the complete second-floor remodel ($30K), the age advantage over every comp ($13–23K), and your yard and outbuilding work, the numbers stack to put you firmly above the comp set's unadjusted range. The Jeddo Rd outlier is excluded from the weighted value — it's a floor data point, not a ceiling.

If you do list at $440K, pricing sharp and clean is the move. This market is absorbing homes in 3–9 days — buyers are active and motivated. Make sure the listing photos do full justice to the remodel. Professional photography of the second floor and updated bath will be the difference between a bidding situation and a single reasonable offer. You should expect FHA and conventional buyers at this price point; confirm the property appraises cleanly and that any seller concessions are factored into your net.

The biggest unrealized lever you have is the story. Buyers in this market don't often see a 2011-built home that's been fully renovated — they're accustomed to 1988–1998 ranches with deferred maintenance. Lead with the age and the move-in condition in every piece of marketing copy. That distinction is what justifies the premium over 10077 Yale Rd and what makes $440K more than a number — it's a genuinely differentiated product.

Greenwood Township is a low-inventory rural market that is moving fast. The three meaningful comps in your price tier sold in 3, 5, and 6 days respectively — that's not a market where buyers are waiting you out. When the right home hits at the right price, it goes. Demand is real, supply is thin, and that combination works in a seller's favor.

The price trend is upward within this submarket. The most recent relevant comparable (10077 Yale Rd, October 2025) sold at $352K in a pre-renovation ranch — if that home had your build year and condition, it would be in the $420K+ range. The market is not stalling; it's rewarding quality and penalizing deferred maintenance. That trend line points toward your number, not away from it.

Sebastian, the takeaway is simple: your home is worth what the data says it's worth — and the data says the market is with you right now. Low inventory, fast absorption, and a clear buyer appetite for updated, move-in-ready homes in the 48006 zip code are all working in your corner.
