The data supports a list price of $440,000. The weighted reconciliation — anchored by the Yale Rd comps and calibrated for your full bath addition, second-floor remodel, and 2011 build year — lands at $438K. Rounding to $440K is not a stretch; it's a precise, defensible number with room to negotiate while still protecting your net. The $370K prior sale is the clearest proof point: the market valued this structure at $370K as-is. The renovation work is why we're $70K higher.

When you list, presentation is everything at this price point. Rural St. Clair County buyers at $440K are doing their homework — professional photography, a clean listing narrative that calls out the remodel and the bath addition explicitly, and an accurate acreage representation are non-negotiable. FHA appraisability matters: make sure every permit for the bath addition is closed, and any outbuilding improvements are clearly documented. A well-prepped listing in this market does not sit — the DOM data confirms it.

The biggest lever you have left is the story. Buyers in this zip code aren't used to seeing a 2011-built, fully remodeled 1.5-story on 5+ acres at any price. Lead with that. If the listing narrative reads like 'updated 3-bed ranch,' you leave money on the table. If it reads like 'rare 2011-built home, fully remodeled second floor, new full bath, 5 acres, outbuildings' — you attract a different buyer at a different ceiling. Let's get that right before we go live.

Greenwood Township is a low-inventory, high-demand micro-market. There are very few homes in this price tier — and when something right-sized and right-priced hits the MLS, it moves in days, not weeks. The comps average 5 days on market. That's not a fluke — that's structural undersupply in a rural market where move-up buyers have nowhere else to go.

Prices at this tier have been trending upward on a per-square-foot basis. The Yale Rd comps in 2025 both cleared $180/sqft on above-grade GLA, and the FHA-friendly price point ($352K–$440K) sits squarely in the sweet spot for the qualified buyer pool. There's no data suggesting softening — thin inventory and consistent sub-week DOM are the signature of a seller's market.

Sebastian — the takeaway is simple: your home is worth what the data says it's worth, and the data says $440,000 is the right number. You did the work, the market rewards the work, and the timing is as good as it gets in this township.
