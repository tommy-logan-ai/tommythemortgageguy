Inventory pressure

Greenwood Township is a rural market with genuinely thin inventory. In the past 12 months, fewer than three homes in the $350K–$420K range have traded — and that's the entire comp universe. What that means in practice: when a property is priced right and shows well, it moves quickly (18–24 days on average). When it's priced wrong, it sits — and in a market this small, every price reduction is visible to every active buyer.

The directional trend is stable-to-slightly-appreciating. The most recent comp (October 2025) at $352K was a smaller, basement-free home, which tells us the floor is holding. The upper comp at $402K (June 2025) shows real appetite for updated, full-featured rural properties. With your improvements, you're positioned at the top of this market — which is exactly where you want to be, as long as the listing is tight and the documentation is clean.

Sebastian, the takeaway is simple: your home is worth what the data says it's worth — and in this case, the data says the work you put in has real market value. The thin comp set is a challenge we go in with eyes open, but it cuts both ways: low inventory means less competition, and a buyer who wants 5 acres in Greenwood Township has very few options. You're it.
