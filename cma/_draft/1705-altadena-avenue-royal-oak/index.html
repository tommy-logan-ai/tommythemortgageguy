The weighted reconciliation — anchored heavily by the two Best comps at Vermont and Blair — points to a target list price of $393,000. Vermont at $413,610 adjusted and Blair at $370,685 adjusted form the bookends; your home's size, bath count, and condition land you squarely in between, biased toward the upper half because of the square footage advantage.

If you list at $393,000 to $399,000, you position above the value floor established by 334 Marlin and 614 Gardenia (both of which sold in the mid-$330s and adjusted down for your home), while staying below the $420K+ aspirational ceiling — which keeps you in the sweet spot for multiple-offer activity in Royal Oak's current 42-day-or-less sales environment. Going aggressive at $410K is defensible only if your condition is show-ready from day one.

The single biggest lever you have before listing is that one bath. A second bath addition or renovation can add $12,000–$20,000 in adjusted value in this market — if you have the budget and timeline, that conversation is worth having before we set the price.
