The weighted reconciliation lands at $855,000 — anchored by the next-door sale at $791K (adjusted to $803K) and bracketed by the Cripple Creek comp at $982K adjusted. The Haverhill and Fairview solds required large GLA adjustments and carry less weight, but they confirm the floor isn't below the mid-$700s for this area.

If you ever do decide to list: the spread between your best comp (1811 Blue Grass at $791K) and this target is about $64K — entirely defensible given your additional 208 sq ft and the 5-bath match. With 9 active listings competing in the immediate area, pricing at $855K–$870K keeps you competitive without leaving real money on the table. Aspirational pricing above $895K risks sitting in a market where buyers have options.

The biggest data gap here is the square footage discrepancy in MLS records (3,658 vs. 5,200 sq ft). If your home truly is 5,200 finished sq ft — and especially if any of that is a permitted addition — getting a floor plan or assessor confirmation in hand before listing could protect every dollar of that size premium.

The Rochester Hills market in this price tier is active but not frenzied. Five sales within half a mile in under four months tells you demand is real — buyers are transacting in this neighborhood. With 9 active listings competing right now, sellers still have an audience, but pricing discipline matters more than it did in 2021–2022.

The price-per-square-foot range in this immediate area ($155–$220/sqft) is wide, which means condition, finish level, and lot premium are doing heavy lifting in negotiations. Homes that are priced accurately and show well are moving; overpriced listings are sitting. The trend from the comp dates (March through June 2026) shows no meaningful price deterioration — the market is holding.

The takeaway is simple: your home is worth what the data says it's worth. At 5,200 sq ft with 5 baths, you're near the top of the neighborhood's natural ceiling, and the comps confirm there's a buyer in the $850K–$895K range if the home is positioned correctly.
