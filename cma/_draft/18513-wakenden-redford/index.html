The data supports a target list price of $179,000. That figure is the weighted center of six adjusted comps, all of which confirm that your double lot and two-car garage push you above the neighborhood median — not below it. This is a stronger position than Zillow's algorithm reflects, because Zillow doesn't know your lot is double-wide or properly credit your garage.

When you're ready to list, price it at $179,000 and come in clean — vacant, touched up, and ready to show. In a market where DOM is 1–6 days, you don't need to discount to sell fast. You need to be priced right on day one. Overpricing even slightly in this market triggers buyer skepticism, and you don't want to be the house that sat.

The single biggest lever between now and listing day is getting the house vacant and in show-ready condition. Buyers in this price range are often FHA/VA-financed, and appraisers will call out deferred maintenance. A clean, vacant home in 48240 priced at $179K will almost certainly see multiple offers within the first weekend. That's the outcome we're aiming for.
