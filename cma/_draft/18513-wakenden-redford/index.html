Redford Township's 48240 zip code is one of the hottest entry-level markets in metro Detroit right now. Homes are selling in one to six days — not weeks, days — and the list-to-sale ratio is running at 99% to 103%, meaning sellers are routinely getting full price or better. Buyers at this price point are motivated, often paying cash, and competing hard for move-in-ready inventory.

There's no sign of that demand cooling in the near term. The April 2026 Norborne sale — cash, $200K, one day on market — is the most recent data point, and it confirms the trend is holding or strengthening. Entry-level inventory in southeast Michigan remains tight, and Redford's relative affordability vs. neighboring communities keeps funneling buyers here.

Eric — the takeaway is simple: your home is worth what the data says it's worth, and right now that's a number worth acting on. The market is doing the work for you. The only thing standing between you and a strong sale is getting the house vacant and listed.
