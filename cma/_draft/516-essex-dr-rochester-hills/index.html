The data points to $580,000 as the Most Probable Sale Price for your home today — that's the weighted midpoint of a comp set that, once adjusted for size and bath count, clusters tightly in the $520K–$628K range. The outlier on the low end (95 Windsor) is a smaller home that needed heavy positive adjustment; the 240 Parkland sale at $625K is a true ceiling indicator. Your home earns a premium over the floor comps because of the bedroom count and size — and a slight concession relative to the upper end because of the 2-full-bath configuration versus the 3- and 4-bath comps.

If you ever do decide to list: this market is genuinely fast. Median DOM in 48307 is running around 11 days, and properly priced 4-bedroom homes are moving without extended negotiation. Pricing at $589,900 would position you at the top of the range while still inviting competitive activity. Pricing at $599,000 is defensible if your finishes are strong — but risks a price cut after 3 weeks if you don't get traction in the first weekend.

The biggest lever you hold is that 4-bedroom count in a zip code where most comparable dollar-volume sales are carrying 3-bedroom footprints. That's real buyer demand you can lean into. If you've done any updates since we last spoke — kitchen, baths, mechanicals — those move the number upward. Worth a quick conversation whenever you're ready.

Rochester Hills is in a seller's market right now — full stop. With only 32 active listings in 48307 and a median days-on-market of 11, well-priced homes aren't sitting. The broad sold median of $400K across all sizes is misleading because it includes condos and smaller ranch homes that drag the average down. Once you filter to 4-bedroom, 2,500+ sq ft homes in your immediate area, the comp median jumps to the $530K range.

Price appreciation in this corridor has been steady through the first half of 2026. The $625,000 sale on Parkland Drive in May is a new high-water mark for the neighborhood and signals that buyers with real money are active. Rising interest rates haven't killed demand here — they've just sorted out the tire-kickers. The buyers competing in the $550K–$625K range are pre-approved and motivated.

Brad, the takeaway for you is simple: your home is worth what the data says it's worth — and right now, that's a strong number. You're sitting on significant equity in a market that's moving quickly. Whether you act on that now or in a year, it's good information to have.
