$735,000 is the most defensible market value for the home in its current condition. It sits just above the weighted indicated value of $738,600 from the four-comp analysis and just below the CoreLogic AVM of $785,800. The Acadia Court adjusted price of $717,688 — the most reliable single comp — anchors the lower end of the target.

If you ever do list: $749,900 list at this price tier is realistic given the size, bath count, deck, and brick exterior. Expect 30–60 days on market and final sale somewhere in the $725K–$745K range. A pre-list paint refresh and kitchen modernization (countertops + appliances + lighting) could move that needle into the $760s–$780s.

The unfinished walk-out basement is the unrealized lever. ~1,900 sqft of walk-out space, finished tastefully, could add $50K–$70K to value at sale time — and it's the single biggest pre-sale ROI move available to you.

The Stratford submarket is steady and balanced. Homes are moving in a healthy 28-day average — fast enough to confirm real demand, slow enough that overpricing gets punished. Sellers are netting roughly 98% of asking, which is normal for a balanced market with no significant urgency or distress.

The neighborhood has appreciated about 9% over the last two years, and CoreLogic's Q1 2026 forecast is essentially flat. This is not a market where you can throw a number at the wall and let bidding wars do the work — it's a market where the right price moves quickly and the wrong price sits.

Neal, Silvia — the takeaway is simple: your home is worth what the data says it's worth. No more, no less. If you ever decide to sell, the playbook is to price it right out of the gate, not to test high and chase the market down.
