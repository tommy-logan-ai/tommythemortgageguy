If you've been reading national headlines about "the slowdown," that's not what's happening here. Frequently asked questions

What is the median home price in Rochester Community Schools?

$512,000 over the last 12 months, based on 911 closed transactions. The middle 50% of sales fell between $338,000 and $700,000. Median by ZIP: $410K in 48307 (Rochester), $616K in 48306 (Oakland Township), $490K in 48309 (Rochester Hills), and $1,000,000 in 48363 (luxury Oakland Township).

How fast are homes selling right now?

10 days on market at the median, 27 days on average. The list-to-sale ratio is 98.2%, and 37% of recent closings sold above asking price. Well-priced homes are going pending within a week of listing.

How many homes are for sale in Rochester right now?

177 active listings as of May 21, 2026. Add 103 pending sales and you have 280 homes currently in motion across the four district ZIPs.

What ZIP codes are in the Rochester Community Schools district?

The four primary ZIPs are 48306 (Oakland Township and parts of Rochester Hills), 48307 (the city of Rochester proper), 48309 (Rochester Hills), and 48363 (luxury Oakland Township corridor). The district boundary also extends into parts of Lake Orion and Auburn Hills, but those areas overlap with other school catchments.

Is now a good time to buy in Rochester?

The market is balanced toward sellers but not as extreme as 2021-2022. Buyers can win, but you need a fully processed pre-approval, a tight offer, and a willingness to move fast on the right home. A 10-day median DOM means you have days, not weeks, to decide. The flip side: inventory is healthy at 177 active homes, so you are not facing zero choices.

How has Rochester appreciated since I bought in 2018?

It depends on your specific neighborhood and home, but most Rochester Community Schools homeowners who bought between 2017 and 2020 are sitting on $100,000-$300,000 in unrealized equity. The neighborhood-specific math is in the free HomeBot tracker above. It pulls real comps for your address.

Should I refinance my Rochester home in 2026?

If you locked your rate at 5.5% or higher between 2018 and early 2020, the math may still work in your favor, especially for a cash-out refinance given how much your home has appreciated. If you locked at 3-4% during the 2020-2021 refi boom, a rate-and-term refi probably doesn't pencil today, but a HELOC or cash-out for a specific goal might.
